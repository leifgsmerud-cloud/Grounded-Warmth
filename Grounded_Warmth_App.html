The five shifts

1. Stillness over animation

Slow the smile. Slow the nod. Hold the silence. Before responding, let a full second pass.

2. Gaze-hold when you speak

Deferential people break eye contact when they assert. Keep eyes on the person until the sentence ends.

3. Evaluate, don't defer

Thanks so much for pointing that out!
That's a sharp observation.
I really appreciate you taking the time...
Good — let's build on that.
Wow, such a great idea!
Yes. That works.
Thank you, this is so helpful!
Useful. Here's where I'd push it...

4. Cut the softeners

I just wanted to...
I want to...
Sorry to bother you...
[skip it]
I was hoping maybe...
Let's...
Does that make sense?
[silence — let it land]
I'm not sure, but...
My read is...
This might be a stupid question...
One question:

5. Offer before you thank

Lead with an observation, a question of substance, or something you noticed. Save gratitude for specific earned moments. Praise down and sideways freely; praise up sparingly.

High-stakes moments

First introduction

"So happy to be here, really grateful for the opportunity..."
"Good to meet you. I've been watching your work with [X]. Looking forward to what we build."

Asked your view in a meeting

"I'm still new, but maybe I'd just add..."
A beat. Then: "Here's what I'm seeing."

Disagreeing with Alex

Swallow it, or wrap in qualifiers.
"I see it differently. Here's why..." — warm tone, steady gaze.

Receiving senior praise

"Oh, thank you so much, that means a lot!"
"Thank you." Hold eye contact. Pivot to substance or let it sit.

The one-line reframe

Humility about the person.
Confidence about the craft.
